## Summary

During Google Cloud Next (April 22–25, 2026), Bloomberg and CNBC reported that Google would commit up to $40 billion to Anthropic: $10 billion in equity at an approximately $350 billion valuation, with another $30 billion contingent on Anthropic reaching undisclosed performance milestones. The equity announcement came eighteen days after Anthropic had disclosed a separate agreement with Google and Broadcom for multiple gigawatts of next-generation TPU compute capacity to come online starting in 2027. Taken together, the deals give Google the largest total committed exposure to Anthropic of any strategic investor — while Google's Gemini model family competes directly with Claude in the frontier AI model market.

## What Happened

On April 6, 2026, Anthropic published an official announcement confirming a new agreement with Google and Broadcom for multiple gigawatts of next-generation TPU compute capacity expected to come online starting in 2027. The announcement described the arrangement as Anthropic's "most significant compute commitment to date," building on prior TPU expansion deals dating to October 2025. The April 6 release disclosed no dollar figure for the compute commitment; it situated the agreement within Anthropic's November 2025 pledge to invest $50 billion in strengthening American AI computing infrastructure.

On April 24, 2026 — during the final days of Google Cloud Next, an annual infrastructure conference held April 22–25 in Las Vegas — Bloomberg and CNBC reported the financial terms of Google's equity commitment to Anthropic. Google, through its parent Alphabet, would invest $10 billion in cash at a valuation of approximately $350 billion, with an option to invest an additional $30 billion contingent on Anthropic meeting undisclosed performance targets. No joint official announcement from Alphabet and Anthropic was published; the terms were confirmed to reporters by parties close to the discussions. Axios reported the same day, framing the announcement alongside Amazon's $5 billion equity commitment to Anthropic four days earlier (see Event 27).

Google's pre-existing investment in Anthropic totaled over $3 billion across several tranches since 2023, representing an approximately 14% equity stake. The April 24 commitment, if fully exercised at the $30 billion contingent tranche, would bring Google's total invested capital in Anthropic to approximately $43 billion — exceeding Amazon's $13 billion in confirmed equity while differing from Amazon's deal structure, which included a $100 billion forward compute commitment from Anthropic rather than a contingent milestone arrangement.

The valuation of approximately $350 billion used for Google's equity investment is consistent with, if slightly below, the $380 billion post-money valuation at which Anthropic closed its Series G in February 2026. Whether this reflects a renegotiated valuation or is an imprecision in reporting is not confirmed.

## Why It Matters

Google's commitment to Anthropic creates a structural arrangement without close historical precedent in the software industry: Google is simultaneously developing Gemini — the primary direct competitor to Anthropic's Claude in the frontier AI model market — and now the company with the largest total committed equity exposure to Anthropic. The strategic logic differs from traditional minority investment. At $40 billion committed, Google cannot treat its Anthropic stake as a hedge or a portfolio diversification. The investment is large enough to reflect a view that Claude is likely to remain a commercially significant technology that Google must remain positioned in — regardless of Gemini's competitive trajectory.

The week of April 20–24, 2026 established that both Amazon and Google, the two largest cloud providers by AI revenue, have made multi-billion-dollar equity commitments to the same frontier AI lab within days of each other. The parallel investment structure concentrates Anthropic's strategic dependency: Anthropic's two largest infrastructure providers (AWS and Google Cloud) are also two of its largest equity holders and two of the companies most directly competing with its products. Whether this concentration gives those providers meaningful influence over Anthropic's technical direction, partnership decisions, or safety policies has not been publicly examined.

What is not established: the specific performance milestones that would trigger Google's additional $30 billion tranche; whether the compute deal (Google TPUs + Broadcom) is financially independent of or bundled with the equity commitment; the exact dilution implications of Google's and Amazon's parallel equity positions for earlier investors including GIC and Coatue; and how Microsoft, the only major hyperscaler without an Anthropic equity position, will respond to the emerging tri-party structure of Amazon, Google, and sovereign capital backing the same lab.
