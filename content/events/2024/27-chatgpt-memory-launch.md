## Summary

OpenAI began rolling out a memory feature for ChatGPT that allowed the model to remember information across conversations, enabling persistent personalization. The feature marked a shift from stateless AI interactions to persistent AI relationships, raising new questions about data retention and privacy.

## What Happened

On February 13, 2024, OpenAI announced a memory feature for ChatGPT that allowed the model to retain information across separate conversations. Users could tell ChatGPT to remember specific preferences, facts, or instructions (e.g., "I prefer Python over JavaScript" or "My daughter is 3 years old"), and this information would persist across future sessions.

Users were given controls to view, edit, and delete stored memories, and could turn the feature off entirely. A "Temporary Chat" mode was introduced for conversations that should not generate or access memories.

## Why It Matters

The memory feature represented a fundamental shift in AI interaction paradigm — from a tool you consult to a relationship you develop. A ChatGPT that remembers your preferences, family details, work context, and communication style becomes meaningfully different from one that starts fresh each conversation. This had implications for user lock-in (switching to a competitor means losing your AI's context about you) and for privacy (persistent memory creates a detailed profile that could be compromised or subpoenaed). It also previewed the direction of AI assistant development: toward persistent, personalized, long-term relationships rather than isolated interactions.
