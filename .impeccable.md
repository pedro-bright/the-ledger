## Design Context

### Users
Primary: AI-interested readers who want **history, not news** — researchers, journalists, policy people, engineers, and serious hobbyists trying to understand what actually happened (and what we got wrong about it). They arrive curious and skeptical; they stay because the sourcing holds up.

Secondary: contributors submitting source tips or corrections via GitHub. The site must make provenance one click away and contribution obvious without requiring schema knowledge.

Context of use: deliberate reading sessions, not doomscrolling. Desktop and mobile, but the long-read experience on mobile matters — events and thread syntheses can run long.

Job to be done: "Show me what happened, who said what, when we were wrong, and how I can check the sources myself."

### Brand Personality
**Archival, sober, precise.** Curator-led — a single human (Terry Tang) visibly responsible for the editorial framework, not an anonymous aggregator.

Voice:
- Quiet authority. Trust earned through rigor, never claimed through adjectives.
- Epistemically humble. "Wrongness is a feature" — significance upgrades, contestation badges, and belief-decay tracking are visible, not hidden.
- No hype. No marketing gloss. The site should feel like a court record or research archive that happens to be beautifully typeset.

Emotional goals: **confidence** (sourcing is solid), **curiosity** (threads invite further reading), **responsibility** (the reader feels the weight of getting AI history right).

### Aesthetic Direction
**Editorial typography meets data journalism.** Stripe Press / Works in Progress rigor — generous whitespace, careful hierarchy, serif-mono interplay as a pattern — combined with Pudding.cool / Observable information density for timelines, graphs, and cross-references.

Locked in (do not redesign without cause):
- **Dark mode only.** `#0A0A0F` background, `#111118` surface, `#E8E4DF` text.
- **Inter (sans) + JetBrains Mono (mono).** Mono is reserved for metadata: dates, counts, tags, IDs, categories. This contrast is a load-bearing pattern — preserve it.
- **Category colors** are tokens, not decoration: policy=indigo, models=amber, research=emerald, industry=violet, safety=red, culture=pink, open-source=cyan. Always paired with text; never color-only signaling.
- **Primary accent** is `category-policy` indigo (#6366F1) for links, CTAs, focus rings.
- **Significance scale**: landmark=amber, major=sky, notable=dim neutral. Use `text-sky-400` on tinted backgrounds (`bg-sky-400/10`), `text-sky-300` for inline uppercase labels without a background tint — the lighter shade compensates for the missing bg. Don't collapse these to one value without re-checking contrast.

Push toward:
- Longer line lengths and more generous leading on event/thread bodies — this is a reading site.
- Subtle, small-scale animation (fade-in, slide-up); never parallax, never scroll-hijacking.
- Monospace metadata strips, thin 1px borders, understated hover states.
- Dense-but-legible list layouts over card-heavy grids where scanning matters.

Anti-references — if a design change makes the site start resembling any of these, reject it:
- **SaaS landing pages** — gradient heroes, product mocks, "Trusted by" logo walls, emoji value props.
- **Crypto / AI startup sites** — neon glows, animated gradients, glassmorphism, generated orbs, "the future is here" copy.
- **Generic AI dashboards** — chatbot bubbles, sparkle icons, "Ask anything" inputs, "Powered by GPT" badges, stock AI visuals.

### Design Principles
1. **Evidence over polish.** Every claim-bearing element (event, thread, controversy) should feel sourced and inspectable. Provenance is a first-class UI concern, not a footer link.
2. **Rigor over excitement.** No hype typography, no hero gradients, no sparkles. If it would look at home on a research-paper preprint, it belongs here.
3. **Information density with breathing room.** Dense where density aids comprehension (timelines, metadata strips, cross-references); spacious where reading fatigue matters (event bodies, thread syntheses).
4. **Designed to last a decade.** Favor timeless editorial patterns over trend-driven styling. A visitor in 2035 should read this as rigorous, not dated.
5. **Contestability is visible.** Significance level, confidence, contestation status, and revision history are core design elements — never afterthoughts. The site's thesis depends on making "we might be wrong" a feature you can see.

### Accessibility Commitments
- **Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.** Fade-ins, slide-ups, scroll-triggered animations must disable cleanly. Motion is never load-bearing for meaning.
- **Color-blind safe.** Category and status color is always paired with text labels. Never use color alone to signal category, confidence, contestation, or significance.
- **Long-read optimized.** Target ~65ch measure on body copy, strong heading hierarchy, generous line-height on event/thread pages so readers don't tire on 2,000-word syntheses.
- **Keyboard + focus.** Visible focus-visible rings (already in `global.css`), logical tab order, no keyboard traps in Graph/Explore interactive views.
