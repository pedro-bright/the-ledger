# AI Assistance Disclosure

*How The Ledger uses AI tools, and the boundaries we maintain.*

**Version:** 1.0 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-08

---

## Policy

The Ledger uses AI tools as part of its editorial process. We disclose this use transparently because we believe honest methodology disclosure is more valuable than pretending AI wasn't involved.

## How AI is Used

### Research assistance

AI tools are used to:

- Summarize and organize source material during research
- Identify relevant connections between events, threads, and actors
- Draft initial summaries and outlines for events
- Check for factual consistency across entries
- Suggest related events and thread connections

### Draft structuring

AI tools may generate initial drafts of:

- Event summaries and "What Happened" sections
- Thread arc descriptions
- Controversy position summaries
- Metadata (tags, categories, actor connections)

### What AI does NOT do

AI tools do not:

- Make final editorial judgments about significance, confidence, or contestation status
- Approve or reject challenges, retrospectives, or canon changes
- Determine which interpretations are included or excluded
- Set editorial policy or modify the constitution
- Replace human judgment on any matter where judgment is required

## Human Review Requirements

All content published in The Ledger, regardless of how it was drafted, must be:

1. **Reviewed by a named human editor** who takes responsibility for the content's accuracy and quality
2. **Marked with `draft_assistance: ai-assisted`** in the event's frontmatter metadata if AI tools were used in drafting
3. **Factually verified** against primary and secondary sources by the reviewing editor
4. **Editorially approved** before publication

## Why This Matters

The Ledger is a project about epistemic honesty — tracking what we know, how we know it, and how our understanding changes. Using AI tools for research assistance while maintaining human editorial judgment is consistent with this mission. Hiding AI use would not be.

We believe that AI-assisted research, combined with rigorous human review, produces better results than either approach alone. The AI is faster at synthesis and pattern recognition; the human is better at judgment, context, and accountability.

## Accountability

The named human editor on each piece is responsible for its content, regardless of how the initial draft was generated. "The AI wrote it" is never a defense for factual errors, biased framing, or editorial lapses.

## Updating This Policy

As AI tools evolve, this policy will be updated to reflect changes in capability and use patterns. All changes will be versioned and recorded in git history.
